What is the course about?

Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA) is about learning how to solve problems in an efficient and structured way. In this course, we study different strategies to design algorithms, measure their performance, and choose the best approach for a given problem.

The ideas from this course are used in almost every area of computer science and technology — from simple programs to large-scale systems that handle huge amounts of data.

What data structures and algorithms have we studied?

We have worked with basic data structures like arrays, strings, linked lists, stacks, and queues, which help us store and manage data in different ways.

We also explored trees, graphs, and hash tables, which are useful for representing relationships, hierarchies, and fast lookups. Along with this, we studied searching, sorting, greedy methods, divide and conquer, dynamic programming, and graph algorithms.
