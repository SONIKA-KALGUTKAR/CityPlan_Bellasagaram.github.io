About Bellasagaram

Bellasagaram, meaning “The Sweet Coastal City,” is a newly proposed urban development inspired by the rich heritage and natural potential of Anakapalli, Andhra Pradesh. Known for its famous jaggery (bellam) market, fertile agricultural lands, and proximity to the Bay of Bengal, this region provides the perfect foundation for blending tradition with modern infrastructure. The city’s name represents this fusion — “Bellam” symbolizing Anakapalli’s sweetness and agricultural identity, and “Sagara” reflecting its sea-based growth through ports and coastal industries. The vision is to transform the area into a sustainable, smart, and economically vibrant coastal city that preserves culture while embracing progress.

Bellasagaram’s development model focuses on the integration of ports, fisheries, recycling industries, cold storage, and solar energy systems. By utilizing its coastal advantage, the city aims to boost exports, generate employment, and attract investments through efficient transport and industrial networks. Solar-powered operations, marine-based industries, and value-added fisheries create a self-sustaining revenue ecosystem, ensuring long-term economic stability. This transformation establishes Bellasagaram as a model marine-industrial hub, where industry, environment, and livelihood thrive together in perfect balance.
